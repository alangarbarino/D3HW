Correlation Between Smoking and Obesity by State

There is a direct correlation between the percentage of obesity and the percentage of smokers in state populations. Census information is insufficient to draw conclusions on causality. However, the direct correlation does not support common conceptions that smoking tobacco suppresses appetite.

Additional research on smoking volume and frequency, substances smoked and delivery method would be helpful in better understanding the relationship between obesity and smoking.
